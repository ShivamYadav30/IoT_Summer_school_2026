QUESTION-30: Explain I2C protocol with respect to IoT sensor communication. What are SDA and SCL lines? How does I2C addressing work? Name 3 common IoT sensors that use I2C. 

I2C (Inter-Integrated Circuit) is a serial communication protocol used
to connect a microcontroller (such as an Arduino or ESP32) with multiple
sensors and devices using only two wires. It is widely used in IoT
because it reduces wiring complexity and allows many devices to
communicate over the same bus.

SDA and SCL Lines

• SDA (Serial Data Line): Transfers data between the master and slave
devices. • SCL (Serial Clock Line): Carries the clock signal generated
by the master to synchronize data transmission.

Both SDA and SCL lines require pull-up resistors because they use an
open-drain/open-collector configuration.

How I2C Addressing Works

• Every I2C device has a unique 7-bit (or sometimes 10-bit) address. •
The master device (e.g., Arduino) starts communication by sending the
address of the desired slave device. • If the address matches, the slave
sends an ACK (Acknowledgement) signal. • The master then performs either
a read or write operation. • Since each device has a unique address,
multiple sensors can share the same SDA and SCL lines without conflict.

Example: • Sensor A → Address 0x68 • Sensor B → Address 0x76 • Sensor C
→ Address 0x3C

Three Common IoT Sensors Using I2C

1.  BMP280 – Measures atmospheric pressure and temperature.
2.  MPU6050 – Measures acceleration and angular velocity.
3.  BME280 – Measures temperature, humidity, and air pressure.

Advantages of I2C in IoT

• Requires only two communication wires (SDA and SCL). • Supports
multiple sensors on the same bus. • Reduces wiring and saves
microcontroller pins. • Simple and reliable for short-distance
communication.
